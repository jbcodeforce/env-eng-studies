# Summary: Environmental Engineering Learning Roadmap

## Main Thesis
A structured, 4-level learning roadmap for environmental engineering (EE) that guides self-motivated learners from beginner foundations through professional certification (PE licensure) over 24–36 months. Designed for **5–10 hours/week** of commitment.

## Key Data Points
- **Total timeline:** Months 1–36 (~1,500–1,800 hours total)
- **Level 1 (Beginner):** Months 1–6, ~250–300 hours — MIT OCW lectures, Davis textbook, LibreTexts
- **Level 2 (Intermediate):** Months 7–14, ~350–420 hours — edX/Coursera MOOCs, specialized textbooks, mini projects
- **Level 3 (Advanced):** Months 15–24, ~400–480 hours — EPA Water Academy, LEED certification, specialization tracks
- **Level 4 (Professional):** Months 25–36, ~500–600 hours — PE Environmental exam, real-world leadership

## Key Concepts
- **PE Path:** Fundamentals of Engineering (FE) → Professional Engineer (PE) Environmental is the longest investment (~36+ months) but highest credential value
- **Specialization Tracks:** Water & Wastewater, Air Quality & Atmosphere, Solid Waste & Remediation, Sustainable Design, Energy & Carbon
- **Continuous Learning:** Maintains weekly reading, monthly journaling, quarterly recertification, annual MOOC habits even at professional level
