# Environmental Engineering Education

A structured, 4-level learning roadmap for environmental engineering designed for self-motivated learners committing 5–10 hours/week, spanning from beginner foundations to professional licensure over 24–36 months.

## Overview

The roadmap divides EE education into four progressive levels:

| Level | Focus | Duration | Hours |
|-------|-------|----------|-------|
| Beginner | Foundations | Months 1–6 | 250–300 |
| Intermediate | Core Competencies | Months 7–14 | 350–420 |
| Advanced | Specialization | Months 15–24 | 400–480 |
| Professional | Certification & Leadership | Months 25–36 | 500–600 |

## Key Components

- **Foundational coursework** via MIT OCW Environmental Engineering, Davis textbook, and LibreTexts
- **MOOC specializations** on edX (Georgia Tech, CU Boulder) and Coursera (TAMU)
- **Specialization tracks:** Water & Wastewater, Air Quality & Atmosphere, Solid Waste & Remediation, Sustainable Design, Energy & Carbon
- **Professional certifications:** PE Environmental, LEED AP, CEM, NACE Corrosion Engineer
- **Continuous learning cycle:** Weekly reading, monthly journaling, quarterly recertification, annual MOOC

## Certification Pathways

- **PE Environmental (NCEES):** Longest investment (~36+ months) but highest credential value — requires FE → PE exams
- **LEED:** Green Associate → AP (WBD) for green building
- **CEM:** Certified Energy Manager for energy efficiency
- **NACE:** Corrosion Engineer for water infrastructure
